9:00–9:30pm

Check into MIMARU Namba. Rest up.

10:30pm

Osaka Sep 12 Fri

Kishiwada Danjiri Festival + Dotonbori Food

Morning

Kishiwada Danjiri Festival (30 min south by train). 4-tonne wooden floats at full speed. One of Japan's most intense festivals.

Afternoon

Osaka Castle and grounds — impressive exterior, good for photos. Skip interior for young Aila.

Evening

Full Dotonbori food crawl — takoyaki, kushikatsu, okonomiyaki, Kuromon Market.

Osaka Sep 13 Sat

Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan + Namba

Morning

Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan — world's largest, whale sharks, giant manta rays, glass tunnel. Allow 2–3 hours.

Afternoon

Tempozan Marketplace and Ferris wheel — views over Osaka Bay. Casual lunch.

Evening

Final Dotonbori and Namba walk — last takoyaki and souvenirs before Kyoto tomorrow.

Kyoto — Sep 14–16

Kyoto Sep 14 Sun

Travel to Kyoto + Nijo Castle + Gion Evening

Morning

Check out Osaka. Forward bags to Kyoto via MIMARU luggage service.

Afternoon

Shinkansen Osaka → Kyoto (15 min, ~¥2,900/adult). Check into MIMARU Kyoto Nijo Castle.

Late Afternoon

Nijo Castle — UNESCO, Ninomaru Palace, nightingale floors, gardens.

Evening

Gion district — cobblestone streets, traditional machiya, maiko spotting. Pontocho alley for dinner.

Kyoto Sep 15 Mon

Fushimi Inari + Arashiyama Bamboo

6:30am (EARLY)

Fushimi Inari Taisha — thousand torii gates. Go before 8am for crowds. Aila will love the orange tunnels.

Late Morning

Arashiyama — Bamboo Grove, Tenryuji Temple garden, monkey park on the hill.

Afternoon

Sagano Romantic Train or Togetsukyo Bridge walk along the river.

Evening

Nishiki Market (Kyoto's Kitchen) — tofu skewers, fresh yuba, matcha soft serve.

Kyoto Sep 16 Tue

Nara Day Trip + Kinkakuji

Morning

Nara day trip (30 min Kintetsu/JR) — Nara Park deer feeding, Todai-ji Great Buddha. Buy deer crackers (shika senbei) ¥200/pack.

Afternoon

Back in Kyoto: Kinkakuji (Golden Pavilion) — iconic gold-leaf temple reflected in pond.

Optional

Ryoanji rock garden, Philosopher's Path walk.

Evening

Kimono dressing experience — book in advance (Gion Chakiraku or Yumeyakata).

Tokyo — Sep 17–22

Tokyo Sep 17 Thu

Travel to Tokyo + Shinjuku Arrival

Morning

Check out Kyoto. Forward bags to Tokyo MIMARU (arrives next day).

Afternoon

Shinkansen Kyoto → Tokyo Nozomi (2h 15m, ~¥13,800/adult). Book reserved seat. Check into MIMARU Shinjuku West (carry-on only).

Evening

Explore Shinjuku — Kabukicho, Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane) yakitori, Shinjuku Gyoen.

Tokyo Sep 18 Fri

Asakusa + Shibuya

Early Morning

Senso-ji Temple Asakusa — arrive before 8am. Nakamise shopping street for souvenirs.

Afternoon

Shibuya — Scramble Crossing observation deck (Mag's Park), Harajuku Takeshita Street, crepes for Aila.

Evening

Shibuya Sky or Tokyo Skytree for night views. Dinner at one of the restaurants.

Tokyo Sep 19 Sat

🎢 Tokyo DisneySea (Silver Week Day 1)

All Day

Full day at DisneySea (world's most beautifully themed park, unique to Japan). Book tickets NOW — Silver Week dates sell out months ahead.

Morning Tip

Use Disney app to book Standby+ passes for top rides. Sep 19 = locals start travelling OUT of Tokyo.

Tokyo Sep 20 Sun

Akihabara + Pokémon + Tokyo Tower

Morning

Akihabara — electronics, anime, manga, gaming. Pokémon Center Akihabara for Aila.

Midday

Tsukiji Outer Market — fresh sushi, tamagoyaki, seafood on sticks.

Afternoon

Tokyo Tower (360° views), Shiba Park.

Evening

Roppongi Hills for dinner and city night views from Mori Tower.

Tokyo Sep 21 Mon

🗻 Hakone Day Trip — Mt Fuji + Onsen

Morning

Romancecar express from Shinjuku (~90 min). Book seat in advance.

Midday

Hakone Open Air Museum — outdoor sculpture park with Picasso pavilion. Great for Aila.

Afternoon

Owakudani Valley — volcanic hot spring eggs (kuro tamago), Fuji views on clear days. Hakone Ropeway if weather permits.

Afternoon

Return to Shinjuku by 7pm. Note: September cloud cover may obscure Fuji — don't plan the view.

Afternoon

Pack, MIMARU luggage storage, stroll Shinjuku Gyoen gardens.

Evening

Farewell dinner — kaiseki or shabu-shabu, booked in advance. Splurge night.

JR Pass: Worth It?

No. Total Shinkansen ~¥32,000–35,000/adult. 14-day pass = ¥70,000 (~AUD $720). Only pays off with many day trips. We're set.

IC Card (Suica/ICOCA)

Load on arrival at KIX: ¥15,000–20,000/person.

Works on: Every subway, JR local train, bus, convenience store.

Kids: Aila under 6 rides JR free (bring passport for confirmation).

Top up: Any station or convenience store when running low.

Getting to/from Airports

Arrival: KIX → Osaka (Sep 11)

Nankai Rapid Service to Namba: ~40 min, ~¥970/adult. No transfers needed.

Departure: Tokyo → KIX (Sep 23)

Option 1 (Shinkansen): Tokyo → Shin-Osaka (2h 30m, ¥13,800), then Haruka to KIX (75 min, ¥3,600). Total ~4 hours + transfers.

Option 2 (Flight): Tokyo (HND) → Osaka (ITM or KIX) ~1 hour, ~¥10,000/person. Faster, easier with Aila.

Recommend Option 2 (flight) for speed and less stress on departure day.

💵 Cash Tips

Japan is cash-heavy. Carry ¥20,000–30,000/adult at all times (~AUD $200–300).

Withdraw from: 7-Eleven or Japan Post ATMs (most reliable). Your bank card works fine.

Before departure: Notify your bank of Japan travel dates (Sep 11–23). Prevents fraud locks.

Cards: Visa/Mastercard widely accepted in cities, but always have cash backup.

High-Impact Savings

Skip JR Pass

We don't need it. Our Shinkansen routes cost ~¥32–35k/person. Pass = ¥70k. Not worth it.

Fly out (Sep 23), don't train

Flight Tokyo (HND) → Osaka (ITM) ~¥10k/person. Faster, less stress on departure day. Shinkansen = 4+ hours + transfers.

Konbini meals

Mix fine dining with 7-Eleven rice balls and onigiri. Saves ~AUD $50–100 over trip.
